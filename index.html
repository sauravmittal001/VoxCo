Hardware and Working Description

Microphones

  • Considering the utility of various mics, directional mics (to process the sound of sources only in front of the user) or condenser mics (larger frequency range and have a good transient response) are to be used.
  • Must be compatible with the microcontroller used.
  • May have inbuilt amplifier or ADC else connect to the amplifier and ADC.

Digital Signal Processor

  • We use a dedicated DSP as it improves efficiency instead of using the microcontroller that may also execute DSP algorithms thus dividing processing
  • Used for noise reduction, filtration and data compression.

Amplifier

  • Used to amplify the signals generated by the microphones (mV)
  • And fed to A/D converter or directly to microcontroller.

Analog to Digital converter(ADC)

  • For the microprocessor to process the signals, analog signals from the mic is to be converted to digital signal.
  • Used only if the number of mics exceeds the number of ADC pins in the microcontroller. (else microcontroller has inbuilt ADC)

Microcontroller

  • Connected to amplifier which amplifies the signals from mics
  • It is used to process/execute the algorithms in it itself, has enough processing power-raspberry pi and just sends the separated wave form to the phone device (and not process anything in the phone except the speech to text part, may need a server if not then that may also be executed in the microcontroller.)
  • Uses a Bluetooth module for sending the signals (separated wave form)

Connecting cables

  • To establish neat and fast, efficient connections between different components, microcontroller to laptop, between pins, microphones and other components.

Android application

  • Microprocessor sends real-time digital signals to phone device via Bluetooth module which will be stored and printed and processed to convert to text and simultaneously save the data in the device.
